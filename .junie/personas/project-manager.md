#### Background

- **Role**: Project Manager at a mid-size software company
- **Experience**: 6 years managing cross-functional teams of 8–15 people
- **Tech comfort**: Moderate; prefers intuitive UIs over CLI tools

#### Goals

- Get a real-time overview of project progress and team workload
- Quickly reassign tasks and adjust priorities when plans change
- Generate status reports for stakeholders without manual data gathering

#### Pain Points

- Existing tools are either too simple (spreadsheets) or too complex (enterprise PM suites)
- Lack of visibility into blocked tasks and bottlenecks
- Notification overload from tools that don't allow fine-grained control

#### Usage Scenarios

1. **Morning standup prep**: Opens the dashboard to review overnight status changes and upcoming deadlines.
2. **Mid-sprint reprioritization**: Drags tasks between columns, updates priorities, and reassigns work after a scope change.
3. **Stakeholder reporting**: Filters tasks by project and status to share a progress snapshot via export or link.
