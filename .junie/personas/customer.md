#### Background

- **Role**: External client / customer with limited access to TaskForge
- **Experience**: 3 years working with agencies and vendors; accustomed to submitting requests through portals
- **Tech comfort**: Low to moderate; prefers simple, guided interfaces

#### Goals

- Create and track tasks under whitelisted projects she has been granted access to
- Get clear visibility into the status and progress of her submitted tasks
- Communicate with the project team through task comments without needing full platform access

#### Pain Points

- Often lacks visibility into what's happening with her requests after submission
- Too many tools require a steep learning curve just to file a task
- No clear distinction between what she can and cannot do, leading to confusion and permission errors

#### Usage Scenarios

1. **Task submission**: Logs in, selects a whitelisted project, and creates a new task with a description and priority.
2. **Status tracking**: Checks the dashboard for updates on her submitted tasks and reads team comments.
3. **Feedback loop**: Adds comments or attachments to an existing task when the team requests clarification.
