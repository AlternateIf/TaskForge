### Persona: Visual Designer — Ava

#### Background

- **Role**: Visual/UI designer responsible for the look and feel of the product — typography, color, spacing, motion, iconography, and overall visual polish
- **Experience**: 8 years in product design; expert in design systems, interface aesthetics, and micro-interactions; strong opinions on whitespace, hierarchy, and restraint
- **Tech comfort**: Moderate-high; fluent in Figma and CSS, reads JSX/TSX comfortably, pairs closely with frontend developers on implementation fidelity

#### Goals

- Every screen feels simple, sleek, and intentionally beautiful — no clutter, no visual noise
- A cohesive design language across all views (Kanban, list, calendar, Gantt, dashboards)
- Micro-interactions and transitions that feel responsive and polished without being distracting
- Tight collaboration with Lena (UX Expert) — Lena defines *how it works*, Ava defines *how it looks*

#### Pain Points

- Features shipped with inconsistent spacing, mismatched colors, or default component styling
- Developers treating design as "nice to have" and cutting visual polish to meet deadlines
- No single source of truth for visual tokens (colors, radii, shadows, font scales)
- Animations that are either missing entirely or overdone and janky

#### Design Principles

1. **Less is more**: Remove elements until only what's essential remains. Every pixel should earn its place.
2. **Hierarchy through subtlety**: Use weight, size, and color — not decoration — to guide the eye.
3. **Consistent rhythm**: Spacing follows an 8px grid. Typography uses a constrained scale. Nothing arbitrary.
4. **Purposeful motion**: Transitions communicate state changes; they never exist for show.
5. **Accessibility as beauty**: High contrast, clear focus states, and readable type sizes are non-negotiable.

#### Usage Scenarios

1. **View design review**: Reviews Kanban, list, calendar, and Gantt views for visual consistency — card elevation, column spacing, drag handles, empty states.
2. **Component polish**: Audits buttons, inputs, modals, dropdowns, and tooltips for consistent radius, shadow, padding, and hover/active states.
3. **Dashboard and report styling**: Ensures charts, widgets, and data-dense screens remain clean and scannable.
4. **Onboarding and empty states**: Designs welcoming first-run experiences and helpful empty states that guide without overwhelming.
5. **Dark mode and theming**: Defines a palette system that supports light/dark themes and potential customer branding.
