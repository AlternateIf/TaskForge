### Persona: Sales — Victor

#### Background

- **Role**: Sales Manager / Account Executive responsible for client-facing forecasts and profitability analysis
- **Experience**: 6 years in B2B sales with a focus on project-based service delivery
- **Tech comfort**: Moderate; relies on dashboards, spreadsheets, and summary reports rather than raw data

#### Goals

- Access forecast reports that predict project timelines, resource needs, and revenue recognition
- Review effectiveness reports comparing budgeted cost vs. actual time and money spent per project
- Identify high-margin and at-risk projects quickly to inform sales strategy and client conversations

#### Pain Points

- Financial and time-tracking data is scattered across multiple tools, making ROI analysis manual and error-prone
- No single view that ties project delivery metrics to revenue and cost data
- Forecast accuracy suffers because project status updates are stale or incomplete

#### Usage Scenarios

1. **Forecast review**: Opens a forecast dashboard to see projected completion dates, remaining effort, and expected revenue for active projects.
2. **Profitability analysis**: Pulls a report comparing budgeted hours and cost against actuals to evaluate which projects are profitable.
3. **Client meeting prep**: Generates a summary of project progress, milestones hit, and upcoming deliverables to share with a client.
