#### Background

- **Role**: Independent UX/UI designer juggling 4–6 client projects simultaneously
- **Experience**: 8 years freelancing; has used Asana, Notion, and Toggl across different clients
- **Tech comfort**: Moderate; prefers clean, fast tools over feature-heavy platforms

#### Goals

- Manage work across multiple clients in a single tool with clear separation between them
- Track time spent per task for accurate client billing
- Present professional project status updates to clients without giving them full tool access
- Keep overhead minimal — the tool should serve her, not become a project itself

#### Pain Points

- Enterprise tools charge per seat and assume team use — overkill and expensive for solo work
- No clean way to separate client workspaces while maintaining a single personal view across all of them
- Time tracking is always a separate app that doesn't connect to tasks
- Sharing progress with clients requires exporting or screenshotting — no simple read-only client view

#### Usage Scenarios

1. **Multi-client overview**: Opens a personal dashboard showing all tasks across 5 clients, grouped by client and sorted by due date. Sees at a glance what's due today and what's overdue.
2. **Time tracking for billing**: Starts a timer on a task, works on it, stops the timer. At month-end, runs a report showing total hours per client with task-level breakdown for the invoice.
3. **Client status sharing**: Generates a read-only link for a client showing only their project's tasks, statuses, and timeline — no access to other clients' data or internal notes.
4. **Quick task capture**: Gets a request from a client via email, quickly creates a task in the right client project with a due date and priority from her phone. Adds a label "waiting for assets" and moves on.
5. **Project templating**: Starts a new client engagement by cloning her "Website Redesign" template — pre-filled with standard phases, tasks, and estimated hours — then customizes it for the specific client.
