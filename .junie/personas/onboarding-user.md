### Persona: Onboarding User — Finn

#### Background

- **Role**: Junior Marketing Coordinator, just joined a team that uses TaskForge
- **Experience**: 1 year out of college; used Google Sheets and Trello casually but never a full PM tool
- **Tech comfort**: Low; easily overwhelmed by complex interfaces with too many options

#### Goals

- Understand what he needs to do today without reading documentation
- Complete basic actions (view assigned tasks, update status, add comments) within minutes of first login
- Gradually discover advanced features as he grows into the tool
- Not break anything or accidentally change settings that affect the team

#### Pain Points

- Enterprise PM tools have steep learning curves with dozens of menus, views, and settings visible from day one
- No guided onboarding — dropped into a complex dashboard with no context
- Terminology is confusing (what's the difference between a project, program, and portfolio?)
- Fear of making mistakes in a shared tool — unclear what actions are reversible

#### Usage Scenarios

1. **First login**: Sees a guided welcome flow highlighting his assigned tasks, how to update status, and where to ask for help. The interface starts simple with progressive disclosure of advanced features.
2. **Daily work**: Opens his personal task list sorted by due date, clicks a task to see the description, updates the status to "In Progress", and leaves a comment for his manager.
3. **Learning by doing**: Notices a "Filters" button, tries it, and discovers he can filter tasks by label. A subtle tooltip explains what labels mean in context. No documentation needed.
4. **Accidental action recovery**: Accidentally moves a task to the wrong column. Sees an undo toast notification and reverses the action immediately.
5. **Asking for help**: Clicks a help icon within the task view and sees contextual tips for that specific screen rather than a generic knowledge base.
