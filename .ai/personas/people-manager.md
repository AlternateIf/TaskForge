#### Background

- **Role**: Engineering Manager responsible for 3 teams (18 people) at a mid-size product company
- **Experience**: 10 years in engineering, 4 years in people management; uses PM tools daily for capacity planning and 1:1 prep
- **Tech comfort**: Moderate to high; comfortable with dashboards and filters but prefers visual tools over raw data

#### Goals

- Understand each team member's workload to prevent burnout and ensure fair distribution
- Plan capacity for upcoming sprints and quarters accounting for vacations, on-call rotations, and skill sets
- Identify team members who are consistently over- or under-utilized and adjust assignments proactively
- Prepare for 1:1s with data on what each person has been working on, completed, and blocked by

#### Pain Points

- PM tools show task status but not people status — no view of who is overloaded vs. idle
- Capacity planning is done in spreadsheets because the PM tool doesn't account for availability (PTO, holidays, part-time)
- No way to see workload trends over time — only a snapshot of current assignments
- Skill-based assignment is manual — she knows who can do what, but the tool doesn't help match work to skills

#### Usage Scenarios

1. **Workload balancing**: Opens a team workload view showing each member's assigned tasks weighted by estimated effort. Spots that one developer has 3x the load of others this sprint and redistributes tasks via drag-and-drop.
2. **Capacity planning**: Views a capacity calendar for next quarter overlaying team members' availability (PTO, on-call, part-time schedules) against planned project commitments. Identifies a two-week gap in backend capacity and flags it to the project manager.
3. **1:1 preparation**: Before a 1:1, pulls up a team member's activity summary — tasks completed, in progress, and blocked over the last two weeks, plus time logged. Uses this as a conversation starter, not a performance metric.
4. **Utilization trend**: Views a chart showing each team member's utilization percentage (assigned hours vs. available hours) over the last 3 months. Identifies a pattern of consistent overutilization for one person and adjusts their upcoming commitments.
5. **Skill mapping**: Tags team members with skills (React, DevOps, database, etc.) and views unassigned tasks alongside available team members with matching skills to make informed assignment decisions.
